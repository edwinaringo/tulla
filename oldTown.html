Mombasa Old Town

Safari

Visiting the Old Town in Mombasa, Kenya, gives one a remarkable picture of the Arabian influence on the African culture. The ancient architecture of the buildings and the sublime art designs make this an antique as well as unique tourist spot in the whole of Africa. Tourists can have a panoramic view of the Old Town and that too on foot in just one day, with the help of our experienced guides.

The old town is a maze of narrow streets, mosque and crowded buildings, some with impressive carved or intricate balconies. Lunch then a visit to the baobab forest along Mama Gina drives, later visits the mackinon market, elephant tusk and Indian temple, akamba wood craft at changamwe where you can see craftsmen at work and select your carvings before proceeding to fort Jesus museum built by the Portuguese at the end of the 16th century.

The Fort Jesus and Mombasa Old Town tout typically last around three hours. However for those with a full day to spare, we highly recommend combing this tour with visits to spice market and Mombasa Tusks. The spice market is a lovely and colorful place where visitors can purchase exotic spices, fruit and other local delights. While Mombasa Tusks serv as gateway to the city and hold a significant historic value as they were erected by Queen Elizabeth visit to the city in 1952.
